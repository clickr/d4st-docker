Configuring Your Own Containers

You can configure your own versions of the D4ST containers through custom docker-compose.yml files.

Full version of this topic TBD.

Short version:
  1. If you haven't already, create your own project to manage your custom container definitions and docker-compose files.
  2. Copy the docker-compose/dfst-build and docker-compose/dfst-server directories into your project.
  3. Copy the directories under containers/ for the containers you want to extend into your own project's containers/ directory.
  4. Rename the directories under containers/ appropriately.
  5. Edit the Dockerfile files for the containers to reflect your changes and extensions.
  6. Edit the docker-compose.yml files as needed to reflect your container details.
  7. Edit the docker-compose/dfst-build and docker-compose/dfst-server docker-compose.yml files to reflect the locations of the docker-compose.yml files for each of your containers.
